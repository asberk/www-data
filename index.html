About Me

I completed my M.Sc. Mathematics in August 2014 at the University of Toronto. My supervisor was Dr. Adrian Nachman, under whom I researched fast computational methods in image processing with applications to medical imaging. Before that, I completed by B.Sc. in Mathematics & Statistics at McMaster Univeristy in Hamilton, Ontario.

During the completion of my degrees, I've developed an insatiable need to understand how humans and computers interpret, parse and understand information. I want to know how information can be represented, processed and communicated. To this end, over the past couple years I've researched applied (computational) mathematics, data and image processing, medical imaging, information theory, learning theory and optimization. In addition, undergraduate research exposed me to mathematical biology, computational fluid dynamics and computational statistics.

Since September, I've been travelling through North America and Southeast Asia. I recently returned to Toronto, Canada, but the wanderlust persists.
